History

Samidha, The Learning Hub, which means “offering made with pure intent,” was founded in 2013 by three college students, Vineet, Vipul, and Ankit. What started as a small campus initiative has grown into a movement. It brings together students and faculty to offer education, mentorship, and hope to underprivileged children.

👁️

Vision

we're aiming to make a real difference by focusing on the education of kids who don't have much and getting everyone involved in the community we're all about those five big things—Curiosity, Competence, Communication, Compassion, and Conviction—making sure every kid gets a well-rounded, meaningful, and empowering education.

Achievements

Since 2013, Samidha's been helping more than 2,500 kids from tough backgrounds by giving them a solid education in the basics, life skills, and culture Beyond just hitting the books, programs that focus on soft skills and building character help people grow in all areas of life. the movement's done a great job of turning student-volunteers into leaders, better communicators, and team players.

🤝

Benefits

Samidha's not just about teaching, it's all about growing together with everyone volunteers get to learn a lot about teaching, they get better at talking, leading, and working with others, they build up their confidence and learn to be patient, and they find happiness in making a difference in kids' lives, all while they keep growing

🎯

Challenges

Samidha’s journey has faced significant hurdles—from convincing parents and managing limited infrastructure to recruiting dedicated volunteers. As the initiative expanded, challenges shifted to ensuring regular attendance, addressing children’s emotional needs, and sustaining volunteer motivation. Through collective perseverance and commitment, Samidha continues to overcome these obstacles and strengthen its mission.

👨‍🎓

Certificates

Samidha is a well-known college society that focuses on educating underprivileged children. Volunteers who teach during their undergraduate years receive verified certificates when they graduate. These certificates recognize their commitment and reflect the leadership, skills, and social responsibility they develop through their active involvement in Samidha's activities.
